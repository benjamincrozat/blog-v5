# Competitive reality and grey-hat risk

- AI Overviews reduce blue-link clicks on informational queries. Pew found users were less likely to click result links when an AI summary appeared, and Ahrefs estimated a ~34.5% CTR drop for position 1 on AI Overview keywords.
  Sources:
  [Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/)
  [Ahrefs AI Overview CTR study](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overviews-reduce-clicks/)

- AI visibility in Google mostly follows organic rankings. Ahrefs found 76% of AI Overview citations came from pages already ranking in Google’s top 10.
  Sources:
  [Ahrefs on AI search overlap](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-search-overlap/)

- Top-3 rankings deserve special attention. Backlinko found the top 3 organic results capture 54.4% of clicks, and the jump from #2 to #1 is especially valuable.
  Sources:
  [Backlinko CTR study](https://backlinko.com/google-ctr-stats)

- Links still matter in practice. Backlinko’s 11.8M-result study found higher-ranking pages tend to have more backlinks, and #1 pages had far more backlinks than positions 2-10. Safer response: publish things worth citing and link internally with intent.
  Sources:
  [Backlinko ranking study](https://backlinko.com/search-engine-ranking)

- Title tactics are not one-size-fits-all. SearchPilot has public SEO tests where title changes increased organic traffic by 10-25%, but other tests decreased traffic by 12-20%. Use competitor patterns and honest testing logic, not fixed formulas.
  Sources:
  [SearchPilot title test: adding “the best”](https://www.searchpilot.com/resources/case-studies/the-best-title-tag-experiment)
  [SearchPilot winning tests PDF](https://www.searchpilot.com/hubfs/7%20winning%20SEO%20A_B%20tests.pdf)
  [SearchPilot losing tests PDF](https://www.searchpilot.com/hubfs/8%20Losing%20SEO%20A_B%20tests.pdf)

- AI-generated content is already common and often cited, but that does not make thin or generic pages safe. Ahrefs found AI-generated content is widespread even on top-ranking pages, while Google still targets scaled low-value content. Use AI to speed up drafting, not to mass-produce filler.
  Sources:
  [Ahrefs on AI content in top-ranking pages](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overviews-cite-ai-generated-content-more-than-human-writing/)
  [Google Search spam policies](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies)

## Grey-hat tactics to recognize and avoid on this site

- Parasite SEO / site reputation abuse:
  can work briefly on high-authority hosts, but it is now explicitly targeted by Google and can trigger manual action.
- Expired-domain abuse:
  still shows up in spammy niches, but Google names it directly as abuse.
- Paid links, rented links, and PBN-style link inflation:
  still influence some SERPs, but they carry the highest durability and reputation risk.
- Fake freshness:
  changing dates without meaningful updates may lift CTR short-term, but it weakens trust and can make the page worse.
- Fake EEAT:
  invented bylines, fake testing claims, or borrowed authority signals are not acceptable.

Use this section for awareness, not imitation.
