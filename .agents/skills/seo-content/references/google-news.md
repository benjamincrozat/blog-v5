# Google News and Discover guidance

Use this reference when a post is timely, uses the `news` category, or the user wants traffic from Google News or Discover.

## Quick split

- `Google News`: for timely, newsworthy coverage and news surfaces such as the News tab or Top stories.
- `Google Discover`: a personalized feed that can show both timely and evergreen content.
- Shared foundation: people-first content, clear authorship, visible dates, strong titles, and high-quality images.

## Blog-specific defaults

- Treat a post as a news candidate only when `categories` includes `news`.
- For Google News eligibility on this site, the post should also be first-party, published, non-commercial, and not sponsored.
- Do not force the `news` category onto evergreen posts just to chase distribution.

## Google News checklist

- Lead with the actual update in the first paragraphs.
- Verify claims with primary sources and link them inline near the claim.
- Attribute sources clearly instead of hiding them in a closing dump.
- Keep the structure tight. Add only the context needed to understand why the update matters.
- Use a clear, non-clickbait headline that matches the page's visible title and metadata.
- Make sure the page has a visible byline plus visible publish time and update time when relevant.
- Use `modified_at` only for meaningful reporting updates, not typo fixes.
- Prefer an original lead image when feasible.

## Google Discover checklist

- Ask whether the angle has broad feed appeal beyond active search intent.
- Titles should be compelling and specific without withholding key context.
- The lead image should be high quality, original when feasible, and at least 1200 px wide.
- Evergreen posts can be Discover candidates without using the `news` category.
- Do not build a fake news angle onto an evergreen post just to look timely.

## Publish decision

- Choose `Search first` when the user problem is steady, query-driven, and better served by a durable explainer.
- Choose `Google News first` when the update is genuinely timely and readers mainly need the current facts quickly.
- Choose `Discover first` when the topic has broad interest, strong framing, and a compelling lead image, even if search demand is mixed.
- Choose `Search + Discover` or `News + Discover` only when the same page honestly serves both.
